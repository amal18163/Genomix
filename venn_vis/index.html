GenomiX: PGHCI Variant Report

Instructions for the Study:

Following is a report that is compares two siblings’ personal genomic information. For the purpose of this study we will name these siblings Jamie and Jessie. Read the explanation below carefully and then study their reports. You will be asked questions about their reports.

The report was created by comparing Jamie's and Jessie's genomes to each other, and to a database of gene variants that are known to be associated with medical conditions or other physical traits.

How to Read this Report:

The report displays a venn diagram of gene variants, where each bubble represents a gene variant. The bubbles on the left of the diagram represent Jamie’s unique variants while bubbles on the right are variants that belong solely to Jessie. Bubbles in the middle represent the variants Jamie and Jessie have in common. Click on a bubble to learn more information about a gene variant. Information including the variant’s name, clinical importance, potential effect, allele frequency, and a summary about the effects it is associated with will appear in the sidebar titled Variant Information located at the right of your window.

You can filter variants by category, health effect, or certainty of evidence by clicking on their corresponding buttons at the top of the visualization. Clicking a filtering button will reveal a drop-down menu of options to filter variants by.

First, please study Jamie’s and Jessie's report carefully. Next, answer the following questions about the report.
